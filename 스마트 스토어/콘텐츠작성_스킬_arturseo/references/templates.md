# Content Templates

Ready-to-use structures for common content formats. Fill in the bracketed placeholders.

---

## Blog Post Template (SEO)

```markdown
# [Primary Keyword + Benefit-Driven Headline]

**Meta description**: [150–160 chars: keyword + what the reader gets + CTA verb]

[Hook: 2–3 sentences. State the problem or make a bold claim that earns the next paragraph.]

[Intro: 100–200 words. Empathize with the reader's situation. Preview what they'll learn. Include primary keyword naturally.]

## [H2: First Key Point — Include Secondary Keyword]

[2–4 paragraphs. One idea per paragraph. Include examples, data, or quotes.]

[Internal link to related content.]

## [H2: Second Key Point]

[Content with practical advice. Use bullet points or numbered lists for scanability.]

## [H2: Third Key Point]

[Content. Include an image or diagram here if helpful.]

> [Blockquote: Expert quote or key takeaway worth highlighting]

## [H2: Common Mistakes / FAQ / Comparison — Pick One]

[Address objections or frequently asked questions. Good for featured snippets.]

## Key Takeaways

- [Takeaway 1]
- [Takeaway 2]
- [Takeaway 3]

[CTA: One clear next step — download, subscribe, contact, or read next post.]

[Internal link to next recommended article.]
```

## Email Sequence Template (6-Part Welcome/Nurture)

### Email 1: Welcome & Promise
```
Subject: [Welcome + what they'll get]
Preview: [Expand on the promise — 80–100 chars]

Hi [Name],

[1 sentence: thank them for joining / downloading / signing up.]

[2–3 sentences: what to expect — frequency, topics, value.]

[1 sentence: your credibility or a quick win they can act on today.]

[CTA: Link to your best resource or a quick-start guide.]

[Sign-off]
```

### Email 2: Problem Agitation
```
Subject: [Name the pain point directly]
Preview: [Hint at the solution]

[Open with a relatable scenario or question.]

[Describe the problem in detail — make them feel seen.]

[1–2 sentences: what happens if they don't solve it.]

[Bridge: "Here's what I've learned about fixing this..."]

[CTA: Read the blog post / watch the video that addresses this]
```

### Email 3: Solution Introduction
```
Subject: [How to solve the problem]

[Reference the problem from Email 2.]

[Introduce your approach / framework / product as the solution.]

[Show how it works in 3 simple steps.]

[Social proof: one testimonial or result.]

[CTA: Try it / Learn more]
```

### Email 4: Social Proof
```
Subject: [Customer name] went from [before] to [after]

[Tell a customer success story — problem, solution, result.]

[Include a specific metric or quote.]

[Connect their story to the reader: "You can do this too."]

[CTA: See more results / Start your own transformation]
```

### Email 5: Objection Handling
```
Subject: [Address the #1 objection — "Is this right for me?"]

[Acknowledge the hesitation directly.]

[Address 2–3 common objections with honest answers.]

[Risk reversal: guarantee, free trial, or easy cancellation.]

[CTA: Take the next step — low friction]
```

### Email 6: Offer / Final CTA
```
Subject: [Create urgency — deadline, limited spots, or bonus expiring]

[Recap the value in 2–3 sentences.]

[State the offer clearly.]

[Urgency element: deadline, scarcity, or bonus.]

[CTA: Primary action — buy, sign up, book a call]

[P.S. line: restate urgency or add a bonus]
```

## YouTube Script Template

```
[HOOK — 0:00–0:30]
[Pattern interrupt or bold statement. Immediately say what the viewer will learn and why it matters. No long intros.]

[INTRO — 0:30–1:00]
"If you're [target audience description], this video is for you."
[Brief credibility: why you're qualified to talk about this]
"Let's get into it."

[CHAPTER 1 — Topic Name]
[Key point with example or demonstration]
[B-ROLL: visual suggestion]

[CHAPTER 2 — Topic Name]
[Key point]
[DATA/STAT: cite source]

[CHAPTER 3 — Topic Name]
[Key point]
[STORY: personal anecdote or case study]

[RECAP — Last 60 seconds]
"So to recap:"
1. [Point 1]
2. [Point 2]
3. [Point 3]

[OUTRO]
"If this was helpful, hit subscribe — I post [frequency]."
```

## Case Study Template

```markdown
# How [Client Name] Achieved [Specific Result] with [Your Product/Service]

## The Client
[1 paragraph: who they are, industry, size, relevant context]

## The Challenge
[What problem were they facing? Be specific — metrics of the "before" state.]

## The Solution
[What did you implement? Describe the approach, timeline, and key steps.]

## The Results
| Metric | Before | After | Change |
|---|---|---|---|
| [Metric 1] | [Value] | [Value] | [+X%] |
| [Metric 2] | [Value] | [Value] | [+X%] |

## In Their Words
> "[Direct quote from the client about their experience]"
> — [Name], [Title] at [Company]

## Key Takeaways
- [What made this work — replicable insight #1]
- [Replicable insight #2]
- [Replicable insight #3]

## Ready to See Similar Results?
[CTA: Book a call / Start a trial / Contact us]
```

## Landing Page Copy Template

```markdown
## Hero Section
**Headline**: [Benefit-driven — what the user gets, not what the product does]
**Subheadline**: [Expand on the headline — who it's for and how it works]
**CTA Button**: [Action verb + outcome: "Start Growing Today"]
**Social proof**: [One-liner: "Trusted by 10,000+ teams" or star rating]

## Pain Section
**Header**: [Name the frustration]
[3 bullet points describing the problem from the user's perspective]

## Solution Section
**Header**: [Position your product as the answer]
[3 features, each translated into a benefit]
- Feature → "Which means you can [benefit]"

## Social Proof Section
[2–3 testimonials with name, title, company, photo]
[Logo strip of notable customers]
[Key stat: "Teams save an average of 12 hours/week"]

## How It Works
1. [Step 1 — simple action]
2. [Step 2 — simple action]
3. [Step 3 — the result they want]

## FAQ
[Top 3–5 objections as questions with clear, honest answers]

## Final CTA
**Header**: [Restate the benefit]
**Button**: [Same CTA as hero]
**Urgency**: [Limited time offer / free trial / no credit card required]
```

## Newsletter Template

```
Subject: [Curiosity + specificity — "3 things I learned about X this week"]
Preview: [Expand — don't repeat the subject]

# [Newsletter Name] — Issue #[N]

**One-line intro**: [Why this issue matters — tie to a trend or event]

---

## 🔍 Big Idea
[Your original take on a trend — 150–300 words. This is the unique value.]

---

## 📌 Worth Reading
1. **[Title]** — [Source] — [2–3 sentence summary + why it matters]
2. **[Title]** — [Source] — [Summary]
3. **[Title]** — [Source] — [Summary]

---

## 💡 Quick Tip
[One actionable tip the reader can use today — 2–3 sentences]

---

[CTA: Reply with your take / Share this newsletter / Upgrade to premium]

[Sign-off]
```
